Statistician and data scientist with a strong foundation in Bayesian and probabilistic modelling, machine learning, time series analysis, and stochastic processes. I believe rigorous mathematics, when applied thoughtfully, can unlock insights that drive real-world change.

I am the author of two peer-reviewed publications spanning non-linear time-series analysis and causal inference methodology. My previous research includes work on Self-Exciting Threshold Autoregressive (SETAR) models applied to Kenya’s NSE 20 Share Index. I am particularly interested in advancing statistical and machine learning methods for real-world applications.

Beyond research, I have contributed to industry as a Machine Learning Collaborator, Data Science Intern, and Compliance Officer, applying data-driven thinking across finance, NLP, and marketing analytics. I am currently a part-time Statistics Lecturer at the University of Nairobi, where I mentor the next generation of analysts.

My long-term goal is to pursue a PhD focused on advancing computational approaches to infectious disease control, an area I have been actively exploring, while building a career that applies data science and statistical modeling to real-world challenges.
